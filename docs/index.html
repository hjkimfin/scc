About

Practitioner insight.
Academic rigor.

Strategy Critique Collective is an independent research group publishing short-form notes and letters on systematic investment strategies — bridging academic research and practitioner reality — translating empirical findings into actionable insight for those who allocate capital.

Our contributors are quant practitioners and researchers who write to apply academic rigor to the questions practitioners face daily: implementation costs that erode theoretical premia, capacity limits that constrain live portfolios, and assumptions that hold in backtests but not in production.

We publish under the series Critical Review of Investment Strategies. Submissions undergo peer review by practitioners in the field.

Submit

Contribute a note

We welcome short research notes (1,500–5,000 words) and letters (under 1,500 words) from practitioners and researchers. We publish research that helps practitioners make better-informed decisions — grounded in evidence, not narrative. All submissions undergo practitioner peer review.

submit.inquiry@strategycritique.org
Submissions must engage a specific strategy, methodology, or empirical claim — not general commentary.
Authors need not disclose their firm or position, but should disclose relevant conflicts of interest.
We accept notes on any systematic or quantitative investment strategy. Factor premia, momentum, alternative risk premia, and ML-based approaches are current areas of focus.
Submissions may be in English. LaTeX or Word both accepted. Response within 3–4 weeks.
No submission fee at this time. Published notes are freely accessible for the inaugural volume.
