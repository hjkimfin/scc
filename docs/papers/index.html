Momentum Crashes and the Illusion of Drawdown Control
J. Kim, H. Park · November 2025

We examine common drawdown-management techniques applied to cross-sectional momentum strategies and show that their apparent effectiveness is largely an artifact of in-sample calibration. Out-of-sample, these controls provide negligible improvement in tail-risk outcomes.

Capacity Constraints in Low-Volatility Strategies: A Practitioner's Note
S. Lee · September 2025

This note quantifies how capacity constraints in low-volatility equity strategies degrade realized returns once AUM exceeds thresholds implied by underlying liquidity — a phenomenon widely acknowledged but rarely measured in the academic literature.

What Fama-French Factors Actually Require of Your Execution Desk
H. Choi, Y. Jung · June 2025

We decompose the implicit execution assumptions in standard Fama-French factor construction and estimate the transaction cost burden that faithful replication imposes — arguing that most reported factor premia are substantially overstated for realistic portfolio sizes.
